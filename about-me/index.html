Hi!

I am a user researcher and facilitator. I work at the intersections of technology and empathy.

I have worked in the tech industry for over eight years. I bring a significant breadth in experience — from user research to web development to expressive arts facilitation.

Over the years, I've worked with several corporates, startups and non-profit organisations. Most recently, I worked at Obvious and helped build Simple.

Helping build a user research practice

During my stint at Obvious, I helped lead our team's user research function for the Simple project for two years (2018–2020). We conducted field studies, evaluative research, generative research and satisfaction surveys on a regular basis.

Through methods like role-playing, journey mapping and storyboarding, and through regular and timely communication, we created a strong user-centred culture in the team.

The product grew from being deployed in 20 healthcare facilities, to a 1000 facilities across countries to manage 300,000+ patients. It won several prestigious design awards, including "Best in Show" at the IxDA awards.

In my practice as a user researcher, I bring in learning from my experiences as a software developer and an expressive arts facilitator. This allows me to work closely with tech products with ease, build strong collaborative relationships with designers and engineers, and to hold space for users and stakeholders with sensitivity.

Building websites

As a web developer, I built websites for organisations like Obvious, Black Baza Coffee and Shoonya Space (2017–2018).

Creating safe spaces

I've trained in a one-year foundation course in creative and expressive arts therapies. Since 2017, I regularly conduct group sessions for self-expression and emotional well-being in Bangalore where I live.

I am looking to work with product teams — especially in the areas of Next Billion Users and EdTech.
